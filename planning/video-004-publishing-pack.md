## Source script

```text
Every home has one drawer that has seen too much.

It starts innocently.
A pen.
A receipt.
A battery you are almost sure still works.

Then one day you open it
and realize you have accidentally built a museum
for tiny unfinished decisions.

The drawer is not really messy because you are lazy.

It is messy because small choices are easy to postpone.
And postponed choices have a way of gathering friends.

The strange thing is,
when you finally empty it,
most of it does not need a dramatic farewell.

It just needs a bin.
A place.
Or permission to stop being your responsibility.

Decluttering is rarely about becoming a new person.

Sometimes it is just admitting
that the rubber band from 2019
has done enough.
```

## Footage checklist

Film vertically if possible. If horizontal, keep the subject centered so it can crop safely to 9:16.

- [ ] Opening drawer slowly; let the mess breathe for a second.
- [ ] Top-down or angled shot of the full drawer contents.
- [ ] Hands picking through ordinary objects: pen, receipt, battery, rubber band, cable, key, old paper.
- [ ] One slightly funny/object-specific hold-up moment — “why do I own this?” energy.
- [ ] Small piles forming: keep / trash / maybe / mystery.
- [ ] Throwing away or recycling a few harmless things.
- [ ] Putting a small number of objects back neatly, but not too perfectly.
- [ ] Final drawer close.

Visual rule: real, ordinary, not Pinterest-perfect. Natural light beats ring light. The drawer can still look lived-in at the end.
